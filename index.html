About the Volatility Index (VIX)

Often referred to as the stock market's "fear gauge", the VIX represents the market's expectation of 30-day forward-looking volatility derived from S&P 500 index options.

  • < 15 (Complacency): Indicates low risk perception, typical in mature bull markets.
  • 15 - 25 (Normal): Standard trading environment with typical market fluctuations.
  • 25 - 35 (Elevated Fear): High uncertainty. Often correlates with market corrections.
  • > 35 (Panic): Extreme distress, capitulation, and potential long-term buying opportunities.

About CNN Fear & Greed Index

A compilation of 7 market indicators tracking investor sentiment. Shows whether fear or greed is driving current stock market pricing.

  • < 25 (Extreme Fear): High investor distress. Historically associated with market bottoms.
  • 25 - 45 (Fear): Investor caution and concern.
  • 45 - 55 (Neutral): Balanced outlook.
  • 55 - 75 (Greed): High investor optimism and confidence.
  • > 75 (Extreme Greed): High complacency. Often precedes market corrections.

About Insider Buy/Sell Ratio

The ratio of insider buy transactions to sell transactions reported on OpenInsider over the trailing 30 days.

  • > 0.50 (High Confidence): Significant buying spikes represent corporate confidence and signal market bottoms.
  • 0.15 - 0.50 (Normal): Balanced activity with typical corporate stock transactions.
  • < 0.15 (Low Ratio): Low buying activity. Insiders typically sell shares for diversification and liquidity reasons.
CENTRAL BANK

Fed Funds Rate

Fed Funds Rate (FEDFUNDS)

The target interest rate set by the Federal Open Market Committee (FOMC) at which commercial banks borrow and lend excess reserves overnight.

Significance: The bedrock of global borrowing costs. High/rising rates seek to cool inflation but constrict liquidity, compressing corporate valuations. Low/falling rates stimulate economic expansion and boost equity multiples.

BOND MARKET

10-Year Treasury Yield

10-Year US Treasury Yield (DGS10)

The yield on the benchmark 10-Year US Government debt security. Often called the "risk-free" rate of return.

Significance: Directly dictates mortgage rates, consumer loans, and corporate debt costs globally. Rising yields decrease the present value of future corporate cash flows, making equities (especially high-growth tech) less attractive compared to bonds.

SPREADS & SENSITIVITY

10Y-2Y Spread (Yield Curve)

10-Year vs 2-Year Treasury Spread (T10Y2Y)

The yield difference between 10-Year and 2-Year US Treasuries. A key gauge of economic expectations.

  • > 0 (Normal): Long-term debt yields more than short-term debt, indicating healthy economic expansion.
  • < 0 (Inversion): Short-term debt yields more than long-term debt. Historically, a yield curve inversion has inverted 12-18 months prior to every major economic recession.
LIQUIDITY

M2 Liquidity Growth

M2 Liquidity Growth (YoY %)

The Year-over-Year percentage change in the M2 Money Supply, which measures the total volume of currency, deposits, and liquid assets in the U.S. economy.

  • > 0% (Expansionary): Indicates expanding liquidity, which tends to support consumer spending, business investment, and asset valuations.
  • < 0% (Contractionary): Indicates tightening liquidity, which can increase borrowing friction, cool inflation, and pose headwinds to stock market multiples.
ECONOMIC ACTIVITY

ISM Manufacturing PMI

ISM Manufacturing PMI (NAPM)

The Purchasing Managers' Index (PMI) is a survey-based economic indicator tracking monthly changes in industrial activity and output.

  • > 50 (Expansion): Indicates manufacturing activity is expanding. High values show strong economic demand.
  • < 50 (Contraction): Indicates manufacturing activity is contracting. Readings below 45 signal rising recession risk.
CREDIT MARKET RISK

High Yield Credit Spread

High Yield Option-Adjusted Spread

The yield difference between below-investment-grade (junk) corporate bonds and risk-free Treasury bonds.

  • < 4.0% (Low Stress): Indicates stable business conditions, high market confidence, and easy borrowing access.
  • 4.0% - 6.0% (Moderate Stress): Indicates tightening financial conditions and rising corporate funding pressures.
  • > 6.0% (High Stress): Indicates significant credit distress, rising default expectations, and looming economic downturns.
MARKET INTERNALS

S&P 500 Breadth (% > 200 SMA)

S&P 500 Breadth (% > 200-day SMA)

The percentage of S&P 500 stocks trading above their 200-day simple moving average. A key measure of stock market participation and internal strength.

  • > 70% (Bullish Support): Indicates broad-based bullish support. Rallies with high participation are healthier and more sustainable.
  • 30% - 70% (Neutral): Normal market conditions with mixed individual stock trends.
  • < 30% (Capitulation/Weakness): Suggests major capitulation or severe systemic weakness. Often indicates oversold market bottoms or ongoing bear markets.
